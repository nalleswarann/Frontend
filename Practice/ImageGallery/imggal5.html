Lake

A lake is a sizable inland body of standing water, typically freshwater, that occupies a basin surrounded by land. Lakes can be formed through various geological processes, including tectonic activity, glacial movements, volcanic activity, and river erosion. They play a crucial role in the ecosystem by providing habitat for diverse flora and fauna, serving as sources of freshwater for human consumption, agriculture, and industry, and offering recreational opportunities such as fishing, boating, and swimming. Lakes can vary greatly in size, depth, and ecological characteristics, influencing the types of organisms they support.
